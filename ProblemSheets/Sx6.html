Solution We start by drawing the tree in order to help us visualise the process.
  1. (a)
    ​

    At t=0t=0 we have

    ℱ0={∅︀,Ω}.\mathcal{F}_{0}=\{\emptyset,\Omega\}.

    At t=1t=1, the filtration is

    ℱ1={∅︀,Ω,{ω1,ω2,ω3,ω4}⏟A11,{ω5,ω6,ω7,ω8}⏟A12,}.\mathcal{F}_{1}=\{\emptyset,\Omega,\underbrace{\{\omega_{1},\omega_{2},\omega_% {3},\omega_{4}\}}_{A_{1}^{1}},\underbrace{\{\omega_{5},\omega_{6},\omega_{7},% \omega_{8}\}}_{A_{1}^{2}},\}.

    At t=2t=2, the σ\sigma-algebra is generated by the set

    {{ω1,ω2},{ω3,ω4,ω5,ω6},{ω7,ω8},{ω1,ω2,ω3,ω4},{ω5,ω6,ω7,ω8}},\{\{\omega_{1},\omega_{2}\},\{\omega_{3},\omega_{4},\omega_{5},\omega_{6}\},\{% \omega_{7},\omega_{8}\},\{\omega_{1},\omega_{2},\omega_{3},\omega_{4}\},\{% \omega_{5},\omega_{6},\omega_{7},\omega_{8}\}\},

    which has the partition set

    A21={ω1,ω2},A22={ω3,ω4},A23={ω5,ω6},A24={ω7,ω8}.A_{2}^{1}=\{\omega_{1},\omega_{2}\},A_{2}^{2}=\{\omega_{3},\omega_{4}\},A_{2}^% {3}=\{\omega_{5},\omega_{6}\},A_{2}^{4}=\{\omega_{7},\omega_{8}\}.

    At t=3t=3, the σ\sigma-algebra is generated by the set

    {{ω1},{ω2,ω4},{ω3},{ω5},{ω6},{ω7},{ω8},{ω1,ω2},{ω3,ω4},{ω5,ω6},{ω7,ω8}},\displaystyle\{\{\omega_{1}\},\{\omega_{2},\omega_{4}\},\{\omega_{3}\},\{% \omega_{5}\},\{\omega_{6}\},\{\omega_{7}\},\{\omega_{8}\},\{\omega_{1},\omega_% {2}\},\{\omega_{3},\omega_{4}\},\{\omega_{5},\omega_{6}\},\{\omega_{7},\omega_% {8}\}\},

    for which the partition is

    {{ω1},{ω2},{ω3},{ω4},{ω5},{ω6},{ω7},{ω8}},\{\{\omega_{1}\},\{\omega_{2}\},\{\omega_{3}\},\{\omega_{4}\},\{\omega_{5}\},% \{\omega_{6}\},\{\omega_{7}\},\{\omega_{8}\}\},

    so that ℱ3=𝒫⁢(Ω)\mathcal{F}_{3}=\mathcal{P}(\Omega).

  2. (b)
    ​

    The risk neutral measures are

    ℚ=(q1q2⋮q8)\mathbb{Q}=\left(\begin{array}[]{c}q_{1}\\ q_{2}\\ \vdots\\ q_{8}\end{array}\right)

    satisfying:

    q1+⋯+q8=1.q_{1}+\cdots+q_{8}=1. (6.1)

    Furthermore, they need to satisfy

    𝔼ℚ⁢(S11+r|ℱ0)=S0,\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}(\frac{S_{1}}{1+r}|\mathcal{F}_{0})=S_{0},

    i.e.

    11.25⁢((q1+q2+q3+q4)⁢6+(q5+q6+q7+q8)⁢4)=4.\frac{1}{1.25}((q_{1}+q_{2}+q_{3}+q_{4})6+(q_{5}+q_{6}+q_{7}+q_{8})4)=4. (6.2)

    Next, it needs to hold that

    𝔼ℚ⁢(S21+r|ℱ1)=S1,\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}(\frac{S_{2}}{1+r}|\mathcal{F}_{1})=S_{1},

    i.e. on A11A_{1}^{1}

    11.25⁢(q1+q2q1+q2+q3+q4⁢8+q3+q4q1+q2+q3+q4⁢7)=6\frac{1}{1.25}\left(\frac{q_{1}+q_{2}}{q_{1}+q_{2}+q_{3}+q_{4}}8+\frac{q_{3}+q% _{4}}{q_{1}+q_{2}+q_{3}+q_{4}}7\right)=6 (6.3)

    and on A12A_{1}^{2}

    11.25⁢(q1+q2q1+q2+q3+q4⁢7+q3+q4q1+q2+q3+q4⁢3)=4.\frac{1}{1.25}\left(\frac{q_{1}+q_{2}}{q_{1}+q_{2}+q_{3}+q_{4}}7+\frac{q_{3}+q% _{4}}{q_{1}+q_{2}+q_{3}+q_{4}}3\right)=4. (6.4)

    From

    𝔼ℚ⁢(S31+r|ℱ2)=S2\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}(\frac{S_{3}}{1+r}|\mathcal{F}_{2})=S_{2}

    we get the 4 equations

    11.25⁢(q1q1+q2⁢12+q2q1+q2⁢7)=8\frac{1}{1.25}\left(\frac{q_{1}}{q_{1}+q_{2}}12+\frac{q_{2}}{q_{1}+q_{2}}7% \right)=8 (6.5)
    11.25⁢(q3q3+q4⁢10+q3q3+q4⁢7)=7\frac{1}{1.25}\left(\frac{q_{3}}{q_{3}+q_{4}}10+\frac{q_{3}}{q_{3}+q_{4}}7% \right)=7 (6.6)
    11.25⁢(q5q5+q6⁢8+q6q5+q6⁢3)=7\frac{1}{1.25}\left(\frac{q_{5}}{q_{5}+q_{6}}8+\frac{q_{6}}{q_{5}+q_{6}}3% \right)=7 (6.7)
    11.25⁢(q7q7+q8⁢6+q8q7+q8⁢1)=3\frac{1}{1.25}\left(\frac{q_{7}}{q_{7}+q_{8}}6+\frac{q_{8}}{q_{7}+q_{8}}1% \right)=3 (6.8)

    If we tried to solve this system of 8 equations with 8 unknowns, we would obtain no solutions, telling us there is no risk neutral measure.

    A quicker way to convince ourselves that that is the case, is trying to find the risk-neutral probabilities (recall that these are the conditional probabilities) on the arrows in the tree. We get at the very top branch S2=8S_{2}=8 that the risk neutral (conditional) probability has to satisfy

    q∗=1+r−d∗u∗−d∗=1.25−78128−78=35,q^{*}=\frac{1+r-d^{*}}{u^{*}-d^{*}}=\frac{1.25-\frac{7}{8}}{\frac{12}{8}-\frac% {7}{8}}=\frac{3}{5},

    which would be ok. If we however consider the node S2=7S_{2}=7 in the lower half of the tree (i.e. on {ω5,ω6}\{\omega_{5},\omega_{6}\}), we get

    q~=1.25−3787−37=2320>1\tilde{q}=\frac{1.25-\frac{3}{7}}{\frac{8}{7}-\frac{3}{7}}=\frac{23}{20}>1

    which is not a (conditional) probability, so a risk neutral measure cannot exists at least on this branch of the tree and therefore not for the whole tree.

  3. (c)
    ​

    The market is not arbitrage free due to the Fundamental theorem of asset pricing, since no risk-neutral measure exists.

  4. (d)
    ​

    We calculate the probabilities on the arrows. Starting with the two top most states of the tree, we have the two equations

    ℙ⁢(ω1)\displaystyle\mathbb{P}(\omega_{1}) =p1⁢p21⁢p31=0.53,\displaystyle=p_{1}p_{2}^{1}p_{3}^{1}=0.5^{3},
    ℙ⁢(ω2)\displaystyle\mathbb{P}(\omega_{2}) =p1⁢p21⁢(1−p31)=0.53.\displaystyle=p_{1}p_{2}^{1}(1-p_{3}^{1})=0.5^{3}.

    Dividing them we obtain

    p311−p31=1⇔p31=0.5.\frac{p_{3}^{1}}{1-p_{3}^{1}}=1\Leftrightarrow p_{3}^{1}=0.5.

    A similar calculation across all branches gives us that all probabilities are equal to 0.50.5.

Problem 6.3.

V^s⁢(ϕ)=𝔼ℚ⁢(V^t⁢(ϕ)|ℱs),0≤s≤t≤T.\hat{V}_{s}(\phi)=\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}\big{(}\hat{V}_{t}(\phi)|\mathcal{F}_% {s}\big{)},\qquad 0\leq s\leq t\leq T.

Vt⁢(ϕ)=Bt⁢𝔼ℚ⁢(VT⁢(ϕ)BT|ℱt),t=0,1,…,T.V_{t}(\phi)=B_{t}\,\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}\bigg{(}\frac{V_{T}(\phi)}{B_{T}}% \bigg{|}\mathcal{F}_{t}\bigg{)},\qquad t=0,1,\ldots,T.

=V^s⁢(ϕ)+𝔼ℚ⁢(G^ts⁢(ϕ)|ℱs)\displaystyle=\hat{V}_{s}(\phi)+\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}(\hat{G}_{t}^{s}(\phi)|% \mathcal{F}_{s}) (V^s⁢(ϕ)\hat{V}_{s}(\phi) is measurable w.r.t. ℱs\mathcal{F}_{s})
=V^s⁢(ϕ)+𝔼ℚ⁢(∑i=1n∑j=st−1ϕji⁢Δ⁢S^j+1i|ℱs)\displaystyle=\hat{V}_{s}(\phi)+\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}(\sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{j=% s}^{t-1}\phi_{j}^{i}\Delta\hat{S}_{j+1}^{i}|\mathcal{F}_{s})
=V^s⁢(ϕ)+∑i=1n∑j=st−1𝔼ℚ⁢(ϕji⁢Δ⁢S^j+1i|ℱs)\displaystyle=\hat{V}_{s}(\phi)+\sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{j=s}^{t-1}\mathbb{E}_{% \mathbb{Q}}(\phi_{j}^{i}\Delta\hat{S}_{j+1}^{i}|\mathcal{F}_{s})
=V^s⁢(ϕ)+∑i=1n∑j=st−1𝔼ℚ⁢(𝔼ℚ⁢(ϕji⁢Δ⁢S^j+1i|ℱj)|ℱs)\displaystyle=\hat{V}_{s}(\phi)+\sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{j=s}^{t-1}\mathbb{E}_{% \mathbb{Q}}(\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}(\phi_{j}^{i}\Delta\hat{S}_{j+1}^{i}|% \mathcal{F}_{j})|\mathcal{F}_{s})
=V^s⁢(ϕ)+∑i=1n∑j=st−1𝔼ℚ⁢(ϕji⁢𝔼ℚ⁢(Δ⁢S^j+1i|ℱj)⏟=0⁢ by definition of r.n.m.|ℱs)\displaystyle=\hat{V}_{s}(\phi)+\sum_{i=1}^{n}\sum_{j=s}^{t-1}\mathbb{E}_{% \mathbb{Q}}(\phi_{j}^{i}\underbrace{\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}(\Delta\hat{S}_{j+1% }^{i}|\mathcal{F}_{j})}_{=0\text{ by definition of r.n.m.}}|\mathcal{F}_{s})

This gives us that
V^s⁢(ϕ)\displaystyle\hat{V}_{s}(\phi) =𝔼ℚ⁢(V^t⁢(ϕ)|ℱs)\displaystyle=\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}(\hat{V}_{t}(\phi)|\mathcal{F}_{s})
Vs⁢(ϕ)Bs\displaystyle\frac{V_{s}(\phi)}{B_{s}} =𝔼ℚ⁢(Vt⁢(ϕ)Bt|ℱs)\displaystyle=\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}}(\frac{V_{t}(\phi)}{B_{t}}|\mathcal{F}_{s})
