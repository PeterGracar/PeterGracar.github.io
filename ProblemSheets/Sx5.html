Problem 5.2.

Consider a market model with T=2T=2, i.e. t=0,1,2t=0,1,2, and riskless interest rate r=14r=\frac{1}{4}, and one risky asset whose prices are described by the following diagram:

(Do not use decimal approximations in calculations.)

  1. (a)
    ​

    Compute a hedge for a European Put option with the strike price equal to K=5K=5.

  2. (b)
    ​

    What is the price one has to pay for this option at the moment t=0t=0?

  3. (c)
    ​

    What would be a fair price for this option to be paid at t=1t=1? Is it a deterministic value or a random variable?

  4. (d)
    ​

    Show that the hedging strategy you calculated is self-financing.

Solution
  1. (a)
    ​

    We first calculate the payoff of the put option:

    The replicating strategy at the node S1=8S_{1}=8 can be quickly calculated to satisfy (ϕ10,ϕ11)=(0,0)(\phi^{0}_{1},\phi^{1}_{1})=(0,0). This also gives us that the value at this node is x1=0x_{1}=0.

    At the node S1=4S_{1}=4 we need to solve the system

    ϕ10⁢(1+r)2+ϕ11⁢6\displaystyle\phi^{0}_{1}(1+r)^{2}+\phi_{1}^{1}6 =0,\displaystyle=0,
    ϕ10⁢(1+r)2+ϕ11⁢3\displaystyle\phi^{0}_{1}(1+r)^{2}+\phi_{1}^{1}3 =2.\displaystyle=2.

    Solving this system we get that ϕ11=0−26−3=−23\phi_{1}^{1}=\frac{0-2}{6-3}=-\frac{2}{3} and consequently ϕ10=4⋅1625\phi_{1}^{0}=4\cdot\frac{16}{25}. The value at this node is therefore x1=ϕ10⁢(1+r)+ϕ11⁢4=815x_{1}=\phi_{1}^{0}(1+r)+\phi_{1}^{1}4=\frac{8}{15}.

    Remark 1: Note that the replicating strategy at this node requires us to hold a negative amount of the underlying stock and a positive amount of the risk free asset. This is because the payoff of the put option (at this node) increases if the stock price falls, so we are required to hold a negative amount of the stock to hedge this fall. For a call option, this would be reversed - the payoff of a call option tends to increase if the underlying stock increases in value, so a hedging strategy will therefore have to hold a positive amount of the stock so that the two move in the same direction.

    Remark 2: The above remark holds for holding long positions of the option. When shorting, the reverse becomes true. For example, a hedging strategy for a short position in a European put option will require a positive amount of the stock, since a fall in the stock price increases the value of the put, and since we are shorting the put our losses would therefore increase. Put simply, a short in a put loses more money if the stock value drops, so to hedge against it we need to hold a positive amount of stock so that our hedge moves in the same direction as the stock.

    We now proceed to calculate the replicating strategy at the node S0=5S_{0}=5, using the values we have calculated at the previous two nodes. We are therefore solving the system of equations

    ϕ00⁢(1+r)+ϕ01⁢8\displaystyle\phi^{0}_{0}(1+r)+\phi_{0}^{1}8 =0,\displaystyle=0,
    ϕ00⁢(1+r)+ϕ01⁢4\displaystyle\phi^{0}_{0}(1+r)+\phi_{0}^{1}4 =815.\displaystyle=\frac{8}{15}.

    Calculating as before, we get ϕ01=−8158−4=−215\phi_{0}^{1}=\frac{-\frac{8}{15}}{8-4}=-\frac{2}{15} and ϕ00=6475\phi_{0}^{0}=\frac{64}{75}. Calculating the value of this strategy at time t=0t=0 we get

    x0=ϕ00+ϕ01⁢5=1475∼0.187.x_{0}=\phi_{0}^{0}+\phi_{0}^{1}5=\frac{14}{75}\sim 0.187.

    Note that the comment and argument from Remark 1 applies here as well.
