Problem 4.1.

In the context of a multiperiod market model with t=0,…,Tt=0,\ldots,T show that if there exists a trading strategy ϕ=(ϕt)0≤t≤T\phi=(\phi_{t})_{0\leq t\leq T} and t∗≤Tt^{*}\leq T such that

  1. (1)
    ​

    V0⁢(ϕ)=0V_{0}(\phi)=0

  2. (2)
    ​

    Vt∗⁢(ϕ)≥0V_{t^{*}}(\phi)\geq 0

  3. (3)
    ​

    𝔼⁢(Vt∗⁢(ϕ))>0\mathbb{E}(V_{t^{*}}(\phi))>0

then there exists an arbitrage. Hint: construct an arbitrage from the given trading strategy.

Solution We require
  1. (1)
    ​

    V0⁢(ϕ)=0V_{0}(\phi)=0,

  2. (2)
    ​

    VT⁢(ϕ)≥0V_{T}(\phi)\geq 0,

  3. (3)
    ​

    𝔼⁢(VT⁢(ϕ))>0\mathbb{E}(V_{T}(\phi))>0.

To build the arbitrage, do the following:
  • •
    ​

    Follow the arbitrage strategy ϕ\phi from t=0t=0 to t=t∗t=t^{*}.

  • •
    ​

    At t=t∗t=t^{*}, cash in the arbitrage opportunity and invest it all in the riskless asset (i.e. the money market account). Therefore, for t≥t∗t\geq t^{*},

    ϕt=(Vt∗/Bt∗0⋮0).\phi_{t}=\left(\begin{array}[]{c}V_{t^{*}}/B_{t^{*}}\\ 0\\ \vdots\\ 0\end{array}\right).
By definition of the arbitrage strategy, there exists an ω∈Ω\omega\in\Omega such that Vt∗⁢(ϕ)⁢(ω)>0V_{t^{*}}(\phi)(\omega)>0 and therefore ϕt\phi_{t} above is not identically equal 0 for at least one ω\omega. At time TT, the value of ϕ\phi is equal to
VT⁢(ϕ)=Vt∗Bt∗⁢Bt∗⁢(1+r)T−t∗=Vt∗⁢(1+r)T−t∗≥0\displaystyle V_{T}(\phi)=\frac{V_{t^{*}}}{B_{t^{*}}}B_{t^{*}}(1+r)^{T-t^{*}}=% V_{t^{*}}(1+r)^{T-t^{*}}\geq 0
and VT⁢(ϕ)>0V_{T}(\phi)>0 on at least one ω\omega by the above.
